Cognate with Middle Dutch eenlijc, eenelijc (Dutch †eenlijk), Old Saxon ēnlīk (Middle Low German einlīk, einichlīk), Old High German einlīh (Middle High German einlich unified, integrated), Norwegian (Nynorsk) einleg, Norwegian (Bokmål) einlig, Old Swedish ēnlig, ēnliker (Swedish enlig unified), Danish enlig < the Germanic base of one adj. + the Germanic base of ‑ly suffix1.

Notes

The Old English doublet forms ānlic and ǣnlic show respectively absence and presence of i-mutation, the latter dependent upon reduction of the stress of the second syllable (compare A. Campbell Old Eng. Gram. (1959) §204.2).
Compare also the rare Old English ānglic solitary, alone (only in two isolated attestations, each glossing Latin unicus in Psalm (Vulgate) 24:16: compare quot. OE1 at sense I.1), apparently < Old English ānga solitary, alone + ‑ly suffix1, after Old English ānlic.
